Maldives – Weather: Peak dry season in January. Expect hot, sunny days ~25–29°C and very low rainfall (~5 rainy days in the month)

travelandleisure.com
, but tourist resort islands are heavily secured and incidents are extremely rare.

South Africa – Weather: Summer heat. In Cape Town and the Western Cape, January is hot and dry, ~27–30°C with virtually no rain

Namibia – Weather: Very hot mid-summer and mostly dry. Most of Namibia is arid desert – January brings 30–38°C daytime highs

Senegal – Weather: Very hot and bone-dry. Interior Senegal reaches about 36°C in January (peak afternoon) with virtually zero rain

The Gambia – Weather: Hot, sunny, dry. This tiny country enjoys virtually rain-free weather in January. Daytime highs on the coast (~Banjul) are around 32°C, with cooler nights ~18–20°C. It’s part of the same Harmattan dry season – you’ll see clear skies and feel dusty desert winds at times. Safety: Medium. Travel Advisory: Level 2 – The Gambia is peaceful and a popular winter-sun destination. The advisory urges a high degree of caution mainly due to petty crime (tourist hustlers or minor theft)
travel.gc.ca
. Stick to the main resort areas and reputable guides (as most visitors do) and you can expect a safe, relaxed trip.

Americas (Central/South America & Caribbean)

. Average highs are a balmy 27–29°C

travelandleisure.com
, but major tourist destinations (Cancún/Riviera Maya, Cabo, PV, etc.) maintain strong security and see millions of visitors safely. Remain in resort areas, avoid wandering isolated places at night, and you’ll likely have a trouble-free vacation.

Panama – Weather: Hot, clear, and dry. Panama’s summer dry season runs Jan–March. In January, weather is sunny across the country with highs around 32°C
jacadatravel.com
. You might encounter a brief shower on the Caribbean side, but generally it’s blue skies and virtually no rain (only ~4 days with any rain)
sunheron.com

travel.state.gov
. Stick to the popular areas (Panama City, Canal zone, Boquete, Bocas del Toro, etc.) and take normal city precautions (especially at night), and you should have a safe trip.

Belize – Weather: Sunny and warm. January is in Belize’s dry season, especially in the north and islands. Expect daytime highs ~28–30°C and minimal rain. Belize City averages only ~50 mm in January and the Cays are typically dry with plenty of sun. It’s a great time for diving the reefs or exploring Maya sites without the heavy rains of summer. Safety: Medium. Travel Advisory: Level 2 – Belize has moderate crime rates. Tourists should exercise caution particularly in Belize City (which has areas of gang crime). Resort areas and islands (Ambergris Caye, Placencia) are much more secure, usually patrolled and safe. Use reputable tour operators and standard precautions; tourism is important to Belize, and popular spots are generally secure for visitors.

Dominican Republic – Weather: Hot and generally dry (winter is the DR’s pleasant season). In Punta Cana and Santo Domingo, highs reach 28–29°C with lots of sun. Rainfall in January is relatively low (brief showers possible, but nothing prolonged). It’s the peak tourism period thanks to warm weather and the end of hurricane season. Safety: Medium. Travel Advisory: Level 2 – The DR is welcoming to tourists, but there is some crime (theft or scams). Resort areas are well-protected; use increased caution if venturing into urban neighborhoods. Most visits are incident-free
travelandleisure.com
. Stick to known tourist zones, and the biggest “risk” you’ll likely face is a rum hangover on the beach.

Jamaica – Weather: Warm and sunny, with January highs around 28–30°C on the coasts. It’s the dry season in Jamaica’s north and west coasts – expect mostly clear skies and just the occasional quick shower. (The south coast can see a bit more rain in winter, but still relatively low.) Safety: Medium. Travel Advisory: Recently improved to Level 2 – Jamaica has areas of high violent crime, but those are far from tourist enclaves. Most travelers stay in resort zones (Montego Bay, Negril, Ocho Rios) which maintain strong security. Remain on resort property or sanctioned excursions, especially after dark
travelandleisure.com
. By doing so, visitors can and do enjoy Jamaica safely – millions visit each year for its beaches and culture without issues.

The Bahamas – Weather: Warm, dry, and breezy. Nassau in January has average highs of 26°C, lows ~19°C, with very low rainfall (~50 mm)
holiday-weather.com
. It’s outside the hurricane season, so you get sunny days and comfortable humidity – perfect for island hopping. Water is a bit cooler (winter), but still swimmable (~24°C). Safety: Medium. Travel Advisory: Level 2 – The Bahamas are overall safe for tourists, especially within resort areas. Nassau does experience some crime; use heightened caution in off-resort areas at night
travelandleisure.com
. Most visits are trouble-free. Keep to well-traveled areas (or your cruise/resort itinerary), and you can expect a safe, relaxing holiday.

Barbados – Weather: Hot and dry. January is in the heart of Barbados’s dry season – you can expect abundant sunshine, only the odd brief shower, and highs about 29°C. It’s a prime time for the beach (and for surfing on the east coast). Safety: Safe. Barbados enjoys a low crime rate and is politically stable. Travel Advisory: Level 1 – standard precautions suffice. The island is known for its friendly locals and safe environment; just take care not to leave valuables unattended on the beach (a good rule anywhere).

Aruba (and Curaçao) – Weather: Very hot and very dry. Aruba and Curaçao, in the southern Caribbean, have a desert-like climate. January highs are around 30°C, and rainfall is extremely low (these islands often see no rain at all in winter months). Constant trade winds keep the heat comfortable. Safety: Safe. These Dutch Caribbean islands are among the safest in the region, with low crime. Travel Advisory: Both are Level 1 (normal precautions)
travelandleisure.com
. Tourists can feel at ease exploring, though petty theft can happen if you’re careless. Overall, Aruba’s slogan “One Happy Island” reflects its safe, tourist-friendly vibe.
